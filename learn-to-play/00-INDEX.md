# Cajon — Starter Songbook

A cajon is a box drum played seated, with the player striking the front face (tapa).
This songbook gives you five groove patterns from simple to intermediate.

## Difficulty key
- ★☆☆ — Beginner: simple repeating strokes, minimal coordination
- ★★☆ — Intermediate: two-hand independence, accents
- ★★★ — Advanced: complex patterns, ghost notes, polyrhythm

## Stroke notation
| Symbol | Stroke | Technique |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **B** | Bass   | Open palm near center of tapa, full resonance |
| **S** | Slap   | Fingers strike upper edge, bright sharp crack |
| **T** | Tip    | Fingertips lightly near upper edge, muted click |
| **H** | Heel   | Heel of hand rests while fingers lift, damp stroke |
| **-** | Rest   | Silence |

## Tunes in this songbook
1. `tunes/basic-groove/` ★☆☆ — Simple 4/4 rock groove
2. `tunes/waltz-groove/` ★☆☆ — 3/4 waltz pattern
3. `tunes/shuffle-groove/` ★★☆ — Swing/shuffle feel
4. `tunes/flamenco-rumba/` ★★☆ — Rumba clave over 4/4
5. `tunes/bossa-pattern/` ★★★ — Bossa nova two-hand groove

## Practice order
Start with `scales/warmup.md` to build single-stroke control, then work through tunes 1→5 in order.
