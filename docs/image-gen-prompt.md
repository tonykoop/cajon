# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, or toolpaths.

## Hero Prompt

A finished Peruvian/flamenco cajón box drum standing upright on a clean neutral seamless studio backdrop, photographed at a 3/4 product-photography angle with a ~50mm lens. The instrument is an upright six-sided rectangular wooden box roughly the proportions of a tall stool — clearly sized to be sat on — built from rich black walnut with warm chocolate grain. Five faces are solid walnut joined at the front corners with a crisp visible finger/box joint, the alternating pins picked out in pale contrasting maple so the corner reads as a woven light-and-dark seam. The two visible side panels carry a recessed CNC-routed geometric inlay panel — a mandala/Celtic-knot motif in maple and yellowheart set flush into the walnut, covering most of the panel with a clean margin of bare wood around the edge. The thin pale front playing surface (the tapa) is a smooth uniform Baltic-birch face, lighter than the walnut body, attached around its perimeter with small evenly spaced brass screw heads that stop a few inches short of the top corners leaving a clean unscrewed slap zone. Satin oil-and-poly finish with a soft sheen reading the grain; the box rests on four small low rubber feet that lift it slightly off the surface. Soft directional studio light from camera-left, gentle falloff, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

A seated player is implied — hands resting on the upper edge of the front tapa as if about to strike the slap zone at the top corner, one palm near the centre for the bass note. Shallow depth of field, environmental storytelling, warm tone, concept visualization only, no text.

## Detail Prompt

Macro close-up of one front corner of the cajón where the finger/box joint meets the inlaid side panel: alternating maple and walnut box-joint pins interlocking cleanly at the corner, beside the flush press-fit edge of a maple/yellowheart geometric inlay seated into the walnut with a hairline-tight glue line. Raking low-angle light to bring out the wood grain, the depth of the routed pocket transition, and the satin finish micro-sheen. Extremely high detail, shallow depth of field, no measurements, no text.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks; no dimension callouts, leader lines, or measurements; no impossible joinery or floating parts; tapa screws must stop short of the top corners; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
